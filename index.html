Be a tester, get paid

The Civic User Testing Group (CUTGroup) is a community of residents of Miami-Dade County
who get paid to test out civic websites and apps

  • How it works

If it doesn't work for you,
it doesn't work!

We are looking for residents from all over Miami-Dade County, who use different types of devices, browsers, and operating systems.

There are technology developers who make websites, mobile apps, and other tools that are made specifically for residents. By being part of the CUTGroup you will help developers make better software that will help make other lives better.

  • Fill out the CUTGroup profile form below to sign up to be a tester. Once you do that, we will send you a $5 VISA gift card just for signing up
  • Once you sign up, you will receive email notifications about new test opportunities. We will ask a few more questions that relate to the technology we are going to test and you can let us know if you are available
  • If and when you are chosen, you will test civic websites and apps. Testing will happen all over Miami-Dade County in public computer centers
  • Once you complete a test, you will receive a $20 VISA gift card

Get Started

We are looking for a diverse group of testers who live in Miami-Dade County. We have done over 15 CUTGroup tests throughout Miami, and now we are reaching out to residents in all of Miami-Dade County to participate. Sign up now and tell your friends!

In order to get the most of the CUTGroup tests, we have to have a fairly scientific take on who is part of the test and how they use technology. This survey has a lot of questions about where you live, what devices you have, and how you connect to the Internet. This helps us get the right mix of people to test a particular application and website.

To get started, fill out the form below. After you complete the form, we will send you a text message to verify your sign-up. Once verified, we will send you a $5 VISA gift card in the mail right away.

About

The Civic User Testing Group is a project of the Code for Miami, a civic organization devoted to using technology to make lives better in Miami. The project is supported by a 2016 Knight Cities Challenge grant provided by the Knight Foundation.

Testing will happen all over Miami-Dade County in different locations.
